What is the point of GNU Screen? Basically once you’re logged in you run the screen command, run some stuff, quit the screen command, and you’ve essentially detached your session.  The next time you log in, you run screen again (reattach) and voila! Back where you left off (and your job has been running in the background all the while!) MAGIC.

The basic flow goes like this:

In your ssh shell type:  

     $screen

Next, Run some commands  (say:)

    $python myPythonScript.py > output &

Then hit CTRL-A followed by D to detach and logout with peace of mind

To restart your session, just use:

    $screen -r

Now, all is well and my wifi is free to drift in and out and my code keeps chugging along.
